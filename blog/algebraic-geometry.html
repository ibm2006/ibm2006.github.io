Algebraic Geometry

Initial notes

Algebraic geometry studies geometric objects defined by polynomial equations. One of its central ideas is that algebraic information about rings and ideals can describe geometric information about spaces, intersections, and singularities.

First Concepts

  • Affine varieties as solution sets of polynomial equations.
  • Ideals as algebraic records of equations defining a space.
  • The dictionary between geometric operations and algebraic operations.
  • Projective space as a natural setting for studying behavior at infinity.

Questions for Future Notes

  • What does the Nullstellensatz say geometrically?
  • How should one think about schemes before learning the formal definition?
  • Where do algebraic geometry ideas appear in combinatorics or number theory?
