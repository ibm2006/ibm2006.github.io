Combinatorics

Initial notes

Combinatorics becomes powerful when counting arguments are connected to structure. The same problem can often be attacked by double counting, an extremal choice, a graph model, or a probabilistic viewpoint.

Main Themes

  • Double counting and bijective proofs.
  • Extremal principles: choose a largest, smallest, or most constrained object.
  • Graph representations of relations, constraints, and interactions.
  • Probabilistic methods for existence proofs.

Questions for Future Notes

  • How do generating functions encode recurrence relations?
  • When is it better to count complements instead of desired objects?
  • How do random graphs connect olympiad combinatorics and research problems?
